Unit 8
8.1 Count and uncount nouns
There are countable nouns. These can be singular or plural.
a book → two books
an egg → six eggs

There are uncountable nouns.
bread
rice

Some nouns are both.
We'd like three ice creams, please. (countable)
Do you like ice cream? (uncountable)

8.2 some, any and a lot of
We use some in positive sentences with uncount nouns and plural nouns.
There is some bread on the table.
There are a lot of oranges.

We use some in questions when we ask for things and offer things.
Can I have some coffee, please?
Would you like some grapes?

We use any in questions and negative sentences with uncount nouns and plural nouns.
Is there any water? I don't know if there is any water.
Does she have any children? I don't know if she has any children.
We haven't got any rice.
There aren't any people.

8.3 would like
Would is the same in all persons.

We use would like in offers and requests.

Positive
I'd like a cup of coffee.
He'd like a cup of coffee.
She'd like a cup of coffee.
We'd like a cup of coffee.
You'd like a cup of coffee.
They'd like a cup of coffee.
('d = would)

Yes/No questions
Would you/he/she/they like a biscuit? Yes, please. / No, thank you.

8.4 How much ...? and How many ...?
We use How much ... ? with uncount nouns.
How much rice is there?
There isn't much rice.

We use How many ... ? with plural count nouns.
How many apples are there?
There aren't many apples.
