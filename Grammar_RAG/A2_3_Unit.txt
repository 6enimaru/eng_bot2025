3.2 Adverbs of frequency
0% never - 50% sometimes - 100% always
(sometimes, often, usually come between never and always)

These adverbs usually come before the main verb.
She never goes out on Sunday.
I sometimes work late.
I often eat in a restaurant.
I usually go to bed at about 11.00.
We always stop work at 6.00.

They come after the verb to be.
She's always late.
I'm never hungry in the morning.

Sometimes and usually can also come at the beginning or the end of a sentence.
Sometimes we go out. We go out sometimes.
Usually I walk to school. I walk to school usually.

Never and always don't come at the beginning or the end of a sentence.
Incorrect: Never I go to the theatre.
Incorrect: I have tea in the morning always.

3.3 like/love + verb + -ing
When like and love are followed by another verb, it is usually the -ing form.
I like cooking.
She loves listening to music.
I don't like studying.
