Be going to
Form: verb to be (am/is/are) + going to + bare infinitive of the main verb
Use
We use be going to:
to talk about our future plans and intentions.
She’s going to do the shopping tomorrow. (she’s planning to ...)
BUT
We use will for on-the-spot decisions.
I’m thirsty. I think I’ll have a glass of water.
to make predictions based on what we see or know.
There are black clouds in the sky. It looks like it is going to rain.
