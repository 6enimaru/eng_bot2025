6.1 Past Simple – spelling of regular verbs
The normal rule is to add -ed.
work → worked
start → started

If the verb ends in -e, add -d.
live → lived
love → loved

If the verb has only one syllable and one vowel and one consonant, double the consonant and add -ed.
stop → stopped
plan → planned

Verbs that end in a consonant + -y change to -ied.
study → studied
carry → carried

6.2 Past Simple
The Past Simple expresses a past action that is finished.

I lived in Rome when I was six.
She started school when she was four.

The form of the Past Simple is the same in all persons.

Positive
I/He/She/It lived in London in 1985.
You/We/They lived in London in 1985.

Negative
We use didn't + infinitive (without to) in all persons.

I/He/She/It didn't live in Madrid.
You/We/They didn't live in Madrid.

Question
We use did + subject + infinitive (without to) in all persons.

When did he/she/it/we/you/they go?
Where did he/she/it/we/you/they go?

Yes/No questions
Did you like the film? No, I didn't.
Did she enjoy the party? Yes, she did.
Did they like the film? No, they didn't.

6.3 Irregular verbs
To be is irregular and has two forms in the past:
be → was/were

Other irregular verbs have only one form in the past:
go → went
can → could
